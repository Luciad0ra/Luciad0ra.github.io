ABOUT ME PORTFOLIO

Project Overview

We learnt from Steel (2007) that almost 50% of college students procrastinate consistently and problematically. It can be caused by the lack of motivation and interest, distractions, and low management skills. Negative consequences of procrastination include anxiety, stress, and remorse, as well as accumulation and low quality of work (Grunschel et al., 2012).

We discovered key insights from 12 interviews and 55 survey responses that our target users, college students, are experiencing negative procrastination behavior, planning fallacy, and difficulties in prioritization. Furthermore, they have no access to learn about their productivity habits and behavior.

We designed chatbot assistants to simplify the task input process and offer personalized suggestions based on our analytics data. With achievements and assistants with different characteristics, we offers immediate feedback and visual rewards to help change behavior and priming.

Psychological Research

Theories Founded in 10+ Key Literatures

Based on our research in key literature, we found significant evidence that chatbots can be a productive tool to improve well-being and reduce stress in non-clinical populations. According to Mihály Csikszentmihalyi’s Flow Theory, setting clear goals, breaking down tasks into just manageable challenges, and receiving immediate feedback are important conditions for achieving flow. Flow theory also suggests that once interactions begin to provide feedback on a person’s skills, they usually begin to be intrinsically rewarding. Based on our research on Persuasive Systems Design (PSD) Models and Behavior Change Techniques (BCTs), Brian Jeffrey Fogg’s theory of Persuasive Strategies suggests that guiding users through tasks and simplifying processes are two useful approaches. That is, guiding users step by step through the target behavior and reducing the process.

02. User Research

We discovered key insights from 12 interviews and 55 survey responses that our target users, college students, are experiencing negative procrastination behavior, planning fallacy, and difficulties in prioritization. Furthermore, they have no access to learn about their productivity habits and behavior.

Key Insights
Planning Fallacy

Our interview participants reported that they usually optimistically underestimate the time needed for the tasks.

Distractions

Procrastination can be caused by distractions (Steel, 2007). According to our interviews, they have difficulties focus on the tasks on hand.

Difficult to prioritize and schedule

Procrastination can be caused by low management skills (Steel, 2007). And according to our surveys and interview, users reported that they have difficulties in prioritize and current apps are inconvenient to schedule and reschedule.

Lack of Motivation

Our interview confirmed our research that lack of motivations may cause passive procrastinate behavior. We got the insights from interviews that our target users are motivated only by deadlines.

Unaware of  Self-Performance

Through in-depth interviews, we found that in the introspective stage, some users are unable to thoroughly introspect herself, for example finding what time she is most productive and is unsatisfied by her quality of work.

03. Solution and design

Planning Fallacy

Our interview participants reported that they usually optimistically underestimate the time needed for the tasks.

Tunneling & Reduction

According to Fogg’s theory of persuasive strategy, guide users to perform the task and simplify the process are two useful methods.

Subtasks

The simple act of unpacking a task can reduce the planning fallacy and easier to complete (Kruger & Evans, 2004)

Offer Suggested Subtasks

Guide user to unpack complicated tasks into just-manageable pieces.

One-Click Add Button

Automatically generate subtasks by detecting keywords in the input. So that user only need to click one button to add.

Distractions

Procrastination can be caused by distractions (Steel, 2007). Interview participants showed difficulties focus on the tasks on hand.

Quote from interviews:
“ It is easy for me to be distracted by phones and people around. ”

Set Goals

Giving users a clear goal in mind help them focus and direct attention which leads to goal-relevant activities (Locke & Lathan, 2002).

Best Practice - Forest

Set a goal for focus time and punish if users leave the app.

To-Do List

Tie focus time to a specific goal to encourage one to be more productive. Users can also see upcoming tasks.

Timer

Reduces distractions by giving users clear goals and notify when they leave the app.

Difficult to Prioritize and Schedule

Procrastination can be caused by low management skills (Steel, 2007). And according to our surveys and interview, users reported that they have difficulties in prioritize and current apps are inconvenient to schedule and reschedule.

Usability Heuristics

Utilize the usability heuristic of flexibility and efficiency of use (Sharp, Rogers, & Preece, 2019).

Best Practice

Both Google Calendar and Apple calendar allowing tapping on blank areas to add tasks and drag and drop to schedule/reschedule.

Drag & Drop

Allowing users to flexibly drag and drop tasks into their calendar and combine calendars into one

Count and Visualize

Automatically counting deferred tasks and visualizing them, give users more incentive to prioritize these first.

Priming

Behavior Change Strategy

Rewarding users virtually has been an effective behavior change strategy (Alqahtani, 2019).

Achievements

We designed assistants that have different personalities and planning methods. The assistants can be unlocked and maintained with 21 days of no procrastination to help users form a good habit.

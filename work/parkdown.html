ABOUT ME PORTFOLIO

ParkDown

An app that delivers timely, updated information about parking availability, regulations, and pricing in a manner that fits into Cornell students’ existing habits and busy lives.

Duration: 3 Months
Role: UX Research & Designer; Collaborated in user research and individually designed an interactive Hi-Fi Prototype
Tools: Figma, PhotoShop

Cornell students struggle to locate legal parking on campus and in the Ithaca area, leading them to incur fines and forgo the many benefits of driving in favor of other transportation methods. In dense areas such as Collegetown, street parking signs have low visibility, and the rules printed on them are often too small to read while driving. In addition, there is no centralized and comprehensive resource for parking in Ithaca that covers all types of parking spaces, including garages, streets, and lots.

User Interview Data Analysis
User Research Insights

Illegal parking

Most Cornell students driver have participated in illegal parking because of visibility and accessibility.

Parking Information

Student drivers are unaware of parking laws in Ithaca because lack of visibility and accessibility.

Parking Trade-Offs

Cons of crowded parking often sway students with cars against driving to a particular location.

Creatures of Habit

Students don’t plan ahead for parking when driving to a habitual location because they are drawn to familiar spots.

Solution Space
Design Selection Process

The design should be easy to interpret and command while driving. Utilizing the solution should not require significant effort and focus, which may detract from user safety while navigating road conditions.

All relevant information about parking in a particular area should be clearly displayed, including hours of operation, legal requirements, and pricing. Students should have the most up-to-date and real-time information about parking spaces, especially if regulations have changed due to special circumstances like construction or events.

Student drivers should be able to get assistance with locating parking without much preparation required before departing. From our research, most student drivers do not think deeply about parking ahead of time, prioritizing ease above most other considerations.

The design should push a notification if the current spot users are parked in will soon become illegal to park in, preventing the occurrence of potentially costly and frustrating parking tickets.
